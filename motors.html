Common questions

Motor & controller FAQ

My scooter has no power — is it the motor or the controller?
It could be either — or it could be the battery, wiring or a blown fuse. The symptoms of a motor fault and a controller fault can look very similar. We run a diagnostic to isolate the exact cause before recommending any repair, so you are not paying for parts you do not need.
What are hall sensors and how do I know if mine have failed?
Hall sensors are small magnetic sensors inside the motor that tell the controller its position so it can deliver power smoothly. When they fail, you may notice jerky or stuttering power delivery, the motor cutting in and out, unusual noises when accelerating, or the motor not engaging at all. In some cases the scooter will throw an error code. Hall sensor replacement is usually much cheaper than a full motor replacement.
My motor is making a grinding or clicking noise — what does that mean?
A grinding noise usually points to a bearing failure inside the motor hub. A clicking or rattling sound can indicate loose wiring, a damaged phase wire or a hall sensor issue. Either way, it is worth getting it checked before the problem gets worse — riding on a damaged motor can cause further damage to the controller.
Can you source a replacement motor or controller for my scooter?
In most cases yes. Common controllers for popular models are available from UK suppliers. Motors vary more by size, voltage and power output — some are readily available from UK stock and others need to be sourced from abroad. Call or WhatsApp us with your scooter model and we will check what is available and at what price before you commit to anything.
Is it worth repairing or should I just replace the whole scooter?
Depends on the scooter and the fault. A wiring repair, hall sensor replacement or controller swap on a mid-range scooter is almost always worth doing. If the motor has physically failed on a very cheap scooter where replacement parts cost more than the scooter is worth, we will tell you honestly rather than take your money.
Do you work on e-bike motors?
Yes — we work on hub motor e-bikes for wiring repairs, hall sensor replacement and motor replacement. For mid-drive systems such as Bosch, Bafang and Shimano Steps we handle electrical faults, wiring and controller issues. Get in touch with your motor make and model and we will confirm what we can do.
